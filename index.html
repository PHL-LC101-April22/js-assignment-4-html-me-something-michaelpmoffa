Mike's Execution Music

Hiss Spun - Chelsea Wolfe

I was told in the summer of 2018 that I would have a paid month's vacation that October. A film was to be shot where I worked and I decided to leave Philadelphia and explore the Pacific Northwest for the first time in 8 years. I found myself at Holy Mountain Brewing on a cold Monday afternoon, enjoying a new sour beer from a just tapped keg. As I talked to my company a loud whirl of feedback took over the space, followed by a thunderous guitar chord. I knew what it was right away; track 1 from Chelsea Wolfe's latest record. I had only listened to it once upon release, but in this particular moment it suddenly all made sense. Sometimes it takes something extra for a person to click with an artwork; for me, on this day, it was realizing how grateful I was to be traveling, and processing the excitement of my trip to follow.

Ok Computer - Radiohead

What more can be said about this album? I was born in 1979, and I could argue before the awful Supreme Court that this is the greatest album to come out in my lifetime. I remember release day vividly; I was living at home about to start college. Most of my friends were packing up and moving away while I had opted to commute for my first year so I could work and save money before transferring to my university's main campus. I felt somehow alone, and this record comforted me in a way that no piece of music has before or since. To look back now 25 years later and see how musicians still site this work as a defining moment speaks to its' importance, and for me personally it's the reason I decided to finally learn how to play guitar.

Loveless - My Bloody Valentine

While released before my tastes were formed, this album was also a personal revolution. I had come to it in high school after hearing an artist I admired mention it in an interview. I won't lie and say I "got it" upon first listen, but as I matured and sought to expand my horizons this record is the beginning of me finding my guitar playing style. I quickly understood that as I began to learn my instrument that technical prowess wasn't what was most important; rather, it was understanding one's own limitations, working within those confines while trying to get better, and establishing a unique sound through creativity. Like Ok Computer I still listen to Loveless regularly, and have even collected guitars with the sole purpose of tinkering with different tunings and leaving detailed notes on the exact sounds I want. It was through the noise of this album that I first was reminded of the beauty of human potential.
